This project involved the design and development of a comprehensive Virtual Reality training simulation aimed at enhancing industrial safety protocols. By creating a fully interactive and realistic virtual environment, users can practice critical procedures and respond to various hazardous scenarios without any real-world risk. The simulation provides immediate feedback, allowing for a deep understanding of safety measures and emergency responses, significantly improving retention and preparedness.

Project Gallery

Key Features

  • **Immersive Scenario:** Recreated a high-fidelity industrial environment, complete with interactive machinery and realistic soundscapes, to simulate real-world conditions.
  • **Interactive Elements:** Implemented intuitive controls for users to manipulate tools, operate heavy machinery, and navigate complex spaces, fostering muscle memory and practical skills.
  • **Performance Optimization:** Achieved smooth frame rates and optimized asset loading to ensure a comfortable and seamless VR experience, minimizing motion sickness.
  • **Real-time Feedback System:** Integrated a system that provides instant visual and auditory feedback on user actions, highlighting correct procedures and identifying mistakes for immediate correction.
  • **Multi-scenario Training:** Developed modules for various emergency situations, including fire safety, equipment malfunction, and chemical spill response, offering comprehensive training.

Technology Stack

  • **Engine:** Unity 3D (primary), leveraging its robust VR development tools.
  • **Programming Language:** C# for all scripting and logic implementation.
  • **SDKs:** Oculus SDK and SteamVR SDK for broad hardware compatibility.
  • **3D Modeling:** Utilized Blender and Maya for creating high-fidelity assets and environmental details.
  • **Version Control:** Git for collaborative development and project management.

Through this project, my goal was to significantly enhance user understanding of complex machinery and emergency protocols, leading to a safer working environment. The feedback received was overwhelmingly positive, with early trials showing a **30% reduction in training errors** and a noticeable increase in trainee confidence and retention. This project stands as a testament to the power of VR in transforming traditional training methodologies.
